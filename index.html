1. Introduction

1.1 The Paradigm Shift: From Dashboard to "Digital Canvas"

The automotive industry is transitioning from a means of transportation to a "Third Living Space." As autonomous driving technologies mature, freeing drivers from mechanical control, the cockpit display has emerged as the central interface for In-Vehicle User Experience (IVX). The recent "Zero-Layer" UI trend, which eliminates physical buttons in favor of full-touch integration, necessitates larger display areas and an explosion of information density. Consequently, the digital cockpit is segmented into the FID (Front Information Display) for essential driving data, the PID (Passenger Information Display) for immersive entertainment, and the CID (Central Information Display) for integrated control. However, this information deluge creates a dilemma regarding "Driver Distraction." Therefore, an "Intelligent Dual-View" capabilityâ€”physically and optically blocking passenger content from the driver's viewâ€”is not merely a convenience feature but a critical safety requirement mandated by enhanced regulations such as Euro NCAP 2026.

1.2 Limitations of Conventional Technologies: Why Not OLED?

Current market-dominant technologies fail to meet the rigorous safety and reliability standards required for automotive applications. First, LCD-based dual-view solutions utilize parallax barriers, which block over 50% of light emission, resulting in poor daylight visibility. Prior works, such as those by BOE, suffered from low contrast ratios and narrow viewing angles. Second, while OLED technology offers perfect black levels, its organic nature presents fatal safety flaws. The most critical issue is Burn-in. The lifespan of the blue organic emitter is significantly shorter than that of red or green, leading to differential aging. Static UIs displayed for long periods leave permanent ghost images, which not only reduce resale value but can also cause information misinterpretation leading to accidents. Furthermore, to prevent thermal runaway, OLEDs employ an ABL (Auto Brightness Limiter) that forcibly dims the screen when the average picture level (APL) is high. This can cause sudden loss of visibility in bright environments, such as driving on snow-covered roads, posing a direct threat to safety.

1.3 The Micro-LED Proposal: System-Level Optimization

We propose an inorganic GaN-based Active Matrix Micro-LED Dual-View Display as the ultimate solution. Micro-LEDs are the only self-emissive devices capable of delivering peak luminance over 10,000 nits without burn-in or ABL limitations. This study transcends simple device fabrication by presenting an integrated system solution encompassing Optical Design, Circuit Driving, and Fabrication Process. By utilizing pixel-level precise optical partitioning, digital PWM driving, and scalable mass transfer, we aim to establish a new technical reference for next-generation cockpit displays.
